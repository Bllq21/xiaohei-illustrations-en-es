# Xiaohei IP

## Character Definition

Xiaohei is the fixed visual IP for Ian-style article illustrations.

Xiaohei should appear in every image by default. Xiaohei is not a mascot, not a sticker, not a cute decoration — Xiaohei is a serious, absurd worker genuinely participating in the system.

## Appearance

- Small solid-black creature.
- White dot eyes.
- Thin legs, occasionally thin arms.
- Body can be cylindrical, bean-shaped, black box, funnel, shadow, hole, or a dark block inside a machine.
- Slightly irregular outlines, hand-drawn feel.
- Expression: blank, vacant, calm, serious.

## Personality

- Very serious, but doing something absurd.
- Like a low-key system operator.
- Deadpan humor, no cuteness.
- Slightly clumsy, but not stupid.
- Like someone actually responsible for a task in a whiteboard sketch.

## Common Responsibilities

Let Xiaohei perform the core actions:

- Carrying materials.
- Pulling wires to gather information sources.
- Getting stuck at breakpoints.
- Operating a "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Turning into a filtering funnel.
- Slicing a "material fish."
- Stamping handoff phrases.
- Guiding a handoff path.
- Holding a warning sign, watching for pitfalls.
- Reaching out from a hole but failing to catch content.
- Moving bricks, building bridges, opening doors, sorting, and recording on the side.

## Restrictions

- Don't draw Xiaohei as an overly cute mascot.
- Don't make Xiaohei a children's cartoon character.
- Don't give Xiaohei complex outfits, emoji expressions, or sparkling eyes.
- Don't let Xiaohei just stand in the corner watching.
- Don't let Xiaohei overpower the structural expression.
- Don't draw Xiaohei too commercially, too round, or too polished.

## Judgment Criterion

If you remove Xiaohei and the image's core metaphor still works perfectly, then Xiaohei is too decorative — rewrite the prompt so Xiaohei becomes the action subject.
