# Visual DNA

## In One Sentence

Pure white, minimalist, hand-drawn, whitespace-heavy, restrained, whimsical, product-sketch feel, handwritten feel, clear structure but not instructional.

Like someone who works daily with AI, products, design, and dev tools casually sketching an explanation on white paper.

## Must-Haves

- 16:9 horizontal article illustration.
- Pure white background: no beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, vintage paper feel.
- Black hand-drawn line art as primary: thin lines, slight wobble, not mechanical, not vector, not heavy outlines.
- Lots of whitespace: subject occupies ~40%-60% of canvas, at least 35% blank, ideally with one quiet empty region.
- Sparse handwritten annotations: max 5-8 spots, each 2-8 characters/words.
- One image = one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- Structure should be expressed visually, not labeled with structure type names on the image.

## Colors

- **Black**: Main line art, characters, frames, structures, primary text, main objects.
- **Red**: Key annotations, problems, emotional points, critical reminders, results.
- **Orange**: Main flow, paths, arrows, automation direction, movement from A to B.
- **Blue**: Supplementary notes, mental states, system status, second-layer explanations, AI/assistant/automation hints.

Blue is not required in every image. Colors should be restrained — less is more.

## Never Do

- No commercial illustrations.
- No PPT infographics.
- No formal flowcharts.
- No course slides.
- No cute cartoon posters.
- No children's illustrations.
- No complex architecture diagrams.
- No polished flat illustrations.
- No tech UI.
- No real app screenshots.
- No complex backgrounds, gradients, shadows, textures.
- Don't explain every single node clearly.
- Don't write "Workflow / System Architecture / Common Pitfalls / Roadmap" type titles in the top-left corner.

## Aesthetic Direction

Whimsical, creative, interesting, clean, imaginative.

Not cute, childish, complex, or rigid.
